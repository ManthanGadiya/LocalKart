1.1 Problem Identification and Definition In today’s world, customers face
difficulty in finding the right product material (e.g., utensils like steel, copper,
aluminium) and nearby reliable shops. They rely on multiple sources like e-
commerce apps, Google search, or manual visits. This makes the process
time-consuming and confusing. Local vendors also lose visibility. There is a
need for a system that not only suggests the right product material but also
shows nearby shop locations where customers can buy them
.
1.2 Problem Description
The current system of shopping is fragmented. For example, if a customer
wants to buy a steel pressure cooker, they have to first decide material, then
manually search local shops or e-commerce platforms. Comparing prices
and availability is difficult. Local shops are not visible digitally. The proposed
system will solve this problem by: • Allowing users to log in and search for
any product (e.g., utensils). • Suggesting the best material options. •
Showing approximate price ranges. • Displaying nearby shop locations using
location-based services (Google Maps API). • Supporting both customer
convenience and local business promotion.

1.3 Fact Finding Techniques • Interview: Asking customers about there
difficulties they face in product material discovery. • Observation: Watching
how people search products in local markets and spend time visiting
multiple shops. • Questionnaire: Collecting user feedback on whether an
app with shop location suggestions is useful. • Literature Review: Studying
apps like JustDial, Google Maps, and Amazon to understand gaps
1.4 Drawbacks of Existing System • No single app provides both product
material suggestions and local visibility. • Customers spend extra time and
effort searching across multiple apps or shops. • Local vendors do not get
online visibility compared to big e-commerce platforms. • Lack of trust factor –
customers don’t know about shop ratings or feedback.

1.5 Scope of Proposed System • Customers can search for product, get best
material suggestions, and see nearby shops. • Shops will be displayed with
location map integration (Google Maps API). • Provide estimated price
ranges and pros/cons of each material. • Future scope: Add multiple product
categories (furniture, electronics, groceries).

2.1 Data Requirements of the System The system requires the following data:
• Product Categories (e.g., Utensils, Electronics, Furniture). • • Features &
Benefits of each product variety (durability, safety, ease of use, etc.). •
Approximate Price Range for each variety. • Nearby Shop Information
(Shop name, location/address, Google Maps link). • User Location (to
suggest nearby shops). Product Varieties (e.g., For Pan → Honeycomb
Coated, Triply, Non-stick; For Kadai → Aluminium, Non-stick, Induction base)

. 2.2 Identify End Users of the System The primary end users of the system are:
• Local Customers who want to buy products (e.g., households, individuals).
• Retail Buyers who compare varieties before purchasing. •
Vendors/Shopkeepers (optional, if they want to register their shop in the
future).

2.3 Input Data to the System • Selected Product Type (e.g., Pan, Kadai). •
Selected User Location or system-detected location. • Optional filters like
Price range or Preferred Material.

2.4 Output Information from the System • List of Product Varieties available
(with features, pros/cons, and approximate price range). • System
Recommendation(e.g., Triply Pan is more durable and safe). • List of Nearby
Shops where the product can be purchased (with shop name, address,
and Google Maps link).
